The Professional Profile

Professional Identity & Research Trajectory

I am a Senior in the Occupational and Technical Studies program at Old Dominion University, where I hold a 3.95 GPA and maintain membership in the Tau Sigma National Honor Society. I serve as the Lead Technical Researcher for The Living Lab, where I connect academic inquiry, bilingual communication, and systems-focused design to questions of administrative burden and housing access. My research centers on the Bandwidth Tax and the Signature Gap, with a focus on how people experience friction, delay, and attrition inside complex compliance environments. Using AI-assisted coding, I have built prototype tools that translate high-friction information into clearer decision pathways. My current work also includes operational experience at UZURV, where I manage high-stakes logistics in ways that closely align with my research on administrative efficiency, coordination, and process reliability.
