The Professional Profile

Professional Identity & Research Trajectory

I am an Occupational and Technical Studies Senior at Old Dominion University (3.95 GPA) and the Lead Technical Researcher for The Living Lab. My work is grounded in nearly 30 years of lived experience navigating federal housing systems, which I transitioned into a formal ethnographic research project in Fall 2025 to mitigate the "Bandwidth Tax" - the 30% drain on cognitive resources caused by administrative friction. Using AI-assisted coding, I developed the RFTA Validator, a diagnostic tool officially submitted to the OpenAI Codex Creator Challenge via Handshake. This tool replaces 20-page "Information Dumps" with bilingual "Hard Stops" to eliminate the "Signature Gap" and prevent program attrition. My current trajectory involves presenting this research at the ODU Knowledge & Creativity Expo on April 22, 2026, and scaling the initiative through the $250,000 Virginia Housing Flagship Training Grant and the $2 million 2026 Housing Affordability Breakthrough Challenge (due May 15). I am preparing for doctoral studies in Human Performance Technology (HPT) to investigate AI-driven regulatory compliance for vulnerable populations.
